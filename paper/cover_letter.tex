\opening{Dear Editor,}

Please consider our manuscript, ``Net-Aware Learned Block Mapping for
Reversible Data Hiding in Binary Images,'' for publication as a Research
Article in the \emph{Journal of Visual Communication and Image
Representation}.

The manuscript addresses a practical gap in binary-image reversible data
hiding: reported capacity often excludes the side information required for
exact extraction and restoration. We introduce a net-aware block-mapping
framework combining globally injective distance-one substitutions,
CNN-based perceptual ranking, and enumerative coding of the active mapping
set, with formal reversibility arguments and wire-level payload accounting.

PPOCP, Huynh--Nguyen, Dong, and the proposed method are evaluated with
identical images, messages, payloads, distortion computation, auxiliary-bit
accounting, and round-trip checks. On matched BOSSbase images, the proposed
method reaches the highest observed net-payload point among the evaluated
implementations. Leave-one-image-out validation covers 13,808 held-out
candidates, and a separate held-out placement experiment reduces mean DRD
from 0.8054 to 0.7663. The study also reports availability, paired inference,
runtime, memory, and grouped steganalysis.

The manuscript fits JVCIR through its combination of image representation,
perceptual distortion modeling, learned content analysis, and experimental
evaluation of reversible visual-data embedding. It establishes net capacity
as an auditable quantity and supplies an exactly reversible implementation
and reproducibility package.

The manuscript is original, unpublished, and not under consideration
elsewhere. All authors approved its submission. We declare no competing
interests or external funding; the study involves neither humans nor animals.

St\'ephane Ga\"el R. Ekodeck is the first and corresponding author. The
co-authors are Vivien Beyala Kamgang, Serge Alain Eb\'el\'e, and Julius
Marcellin Nkenlifack.

Thank you for considering our manuscript.
